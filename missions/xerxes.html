LCARS • Mission Brief

Location: Xerxes IV, Xerxes System

Assigned: Stardate 56828.9

Situation Report

At Stardate 56827.0, Starfleet recieved a distress call from a Federation Scientific Survey team on the uninhabited planet of Xerxes IV in the Xerxes system. Unfortunately, the details of the distress call were apparently lost in transmission. The USS Unity, a Galaxy class starship, was immediately assigned to attend to the crisis, but they experienced a navigational malfunction that they have been unable to remedy quickly enough to continue responding to the situation. As such, the task has been reassigned to the newly deployed USS Horizon, who are the next closest Starfleet vessel to Xerxes.

The Horizon's task is to land an away team on the planet's surface, locate the Scientific Survey team, and return them safely to the Horizon and eventually back to DS9.

Environmental Report

Xerxes IV is an uninhabited planet, though it is rife with non-sentient flora and fauna that have adapted to the planet's occasional harsh ion storms caused by the planet's orbit intersecting with protruding segments of a massive cloud of energized gas. These ion storms have been reported by the Federation Scientific Survey team to be quite the obstacle, as while their survey equipment could be properly shielded against the ion flux, they did not have sufficient materials to shield their communications systems, and thus when the storms hit their peak they would lose long-range communication capabilities for some time. The ion storms can be withstood by humanoids for several hours without any special protective equipment, but for prolonged exposure a specially shielded EVA suit is recommended. Exact reaction to prolonged exposure to the ion storms is thought to vary from species to species but is largely unknown as that is not the nature of the team's project. It is unknown whether the ion storms disrupt transporters, phasers, or thrusters, as none of these technologies have been attempted to be used while an ion storm is active.

The latest predictive model based on the last batch of data sent by the Survey team finds it likely that there is currently an ion storm in progress over the site of the Survey team's outpost. This is thought to be why their distress signal was disrupted and why the outpost has not responded to attempts to establish communication.

All information from this point was added to the mission brief after the mission was completed.

Mission Log

The USS Horizon immediately warped to the Xerxes System upon recieving their assignment. An away team consisting of the following members of crew was assembled:

  • Commander Eskei Ghemor - Medical
  • Lieutenant Boreman - Medical
  • Lieutenant Chloe Clark - Security
  • Lieutenant Cecilia Pierce - Security
  • Lieutenant Jonathan Weissen - Pilot

Due to the conditions of the ion storm raging across a wide portion of the planet, the ship's systems were unable to provide a satisfactory coordinate lock for transporters to be of use, so a shuttle was used to transport the away team to the planet's surface, with Lt. Weissen at the conn.
